\title{Preservers of moment sequences}
\author{Apoorva Khare} %Presenting author only

% The abstract  ***no more than one page***

Call a measure on $\mathbb{R}$ \textit{admissible} if it is non-negative
and admits all moments. We classify all functions on the real line which
when applied termwise, preserve the class of moment-sequences of
admissible measures (i.e., take one such sequence to another). We show
that all such functions are absolutely monotonic -- and conversely -- and
that surprisingly, it suffices to restrict the test measures to three
point masses in $[-1,1]$. This strengthens and parallels a dimension-free
positivity preserver result by Schoenberg
[\href{http://dx.doi.org/10.1215/S0012-7094-42-00908-6}{{\em Duke} 1942}]
and Rudin
[\href{http://dx.doi.org/10.1215/S0012-7094-59-02659-6}{{\em Duke}
1959}], and is joint work with Belton, Guillot, and Putinar
[\href{http://dx.doi.org/10.4171/jems/1145}{{\em JEMS}, to appear}].
