\title{New Connections  between Static Matrices $A$, Zhang Neural Networks, and Parameter-Varying Matrix Flows $A(t)$}

% The abstract  ***no more than one page***

We study recent results that connect classical matrix theory with matrix computations in new ways.\\[1mm]  
Field of values computations lead me to  study  parameter-varying matrix eigenvalue  problems. These concepts helped me solve a century old matrix block diagonalization problem that arose in Quantum Physics in the 1920s and was still open. \\[1mm]
 On the way we need to understand  time-varying matrix problems theoretically and computationally when specifically  approached through Zeroing Neural Networks.  \\[1mm] 
 Zhang Neural Networks (ZNN) are being used today in hundreds of ways in modern engineering, in robot control etc.\\[1mm]
 But their numerical behavior  is barely understood at this time. ZNN methods pose many open problems and form a  new, non-Wilkinsonian branch of Numerical Matrix  Analysis.\\
 In return,   ZNN methods for matrix flows $A(t)$ can help us for the
 first time to solve long standing intractable computational problems
 of fixed entry matrices $A$ such as the matrix symmetrizer problem.
