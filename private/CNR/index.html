Visualisation of solution

April 2024

Here we show the numerical solution to \[ u_t - \varepsilon^2 \Delta u + r(x,y)u = f(x,y,t) \] where the spacial domain is $\Omega:=(0,1)^2$, and the PDE's domain is $(x,y,t) \in \Omega \times(0,1]$. Boundary conditions: $u(\cdot,\cdot,t)|_{\partial \Omega}=0$, and initial condition us $u(x,y,0)\equiv 0$. For the specific example shown where we've $\epsilon=10^{-2}$. The PDE is solved using a $P_2$ Galerkin FEM, and integrated in time using Backward Euler. The implementation is in Firedrake (with irksome for time integration).

For this specific example, we use $r \equiv 1$, and \[ u(x,y,t) = -\big((t + 1)e^{-t} - 1\big) (h_1(x) - 1 - e^{-1/\varepsilon}) (h_1(y) - 1 - e^{-1/\varepsilon}) \] where \[ h_1(x) = \frac{e^{-x/\varepsilon} + e^{-(1.0 - x)/\varepsilon}}{1 + e^{-1/\varepsilon}}. \]
