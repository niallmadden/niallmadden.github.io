Visualisation of time-dep solution.

22 Nov 2023

Here we show the solution to \[ u_t - \alpha \Delta u + b_1 u_x + b_2(t)u_y =0, \] where $(x,y,t) \in (x_0,x_1)\times(y_0,y_1)\times(t_0,T]$, and the boundary conditions and initial condition are given. For the specific example shown where we've taken \[ \alpha=5 \times 10^{-3}, b_1 \equiv 2, b_2(t)=\sin(2\pi t). \] The domain is $(x,y,t) \in (x_0,x_1)\times(y_0,y_1)\times(t_0,T] = (-0.2, 4) \times (0,1) \times (0,2]$. The boundary conditions are \[ u(x_0,y)=u(x_1,y)=u(x,y_0)=u(x,y_1)=0,\] and the initial condition is \[ u(x,y,t_0) = \begin{cases} 1 & x^2+(y-0.2)^2 \leq (0.1)^2\\ 0 & \text{ otherwise}. \end{cases} \] The PDE is solved using a $P_2$ Galerkin FEM with the implicit midpoint method in time. The implementation is in Firedrake (with irksome for time integration). It is notable that there is an initial oscillation in the solution. This is due to the jump discontinuity in the initial condition.
